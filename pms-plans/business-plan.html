1) Executive summary

Problem: Small PM firms juggle too many tools and manual workflows (inspections, maintenance triage, vendor coordination, billing, leasing, docs, tenant comms). Data is fragmented, follow-through is inconsistent, and labor hours leak everywhere.

Solution: A refined, pragmatic property management suite where inspections are the operational backbone. The product turns inspection findings into actionable maintenance scope and produces an AI-assisted estimate/work plan (cost range + timeline + assumptions + questions). This reduces surprises, speeds vendor dispatch, improves owner trust, and creates a clearer operating cadence.

Business model: Subscription SaaS with per-unit pricing + optional add-ons (SMS, e-sign envelopes, advanced AI).

Near-term goal: Reach $10k MRR by landing a small number of firms (e.g., 5–20 firms depending on doors/pricing).

2) Vision, wedge, and product thesis

Vision

Become the operations layer for small-to-mid PMs: a single system that captures property reality (inspections + maintenance), automates follow-through, and integrates with payments/accounting.

Wedge (why switch?)

Predictive maintenance workflow: Inspection items → structured action fields (severity/issue type/measurement/notes) → estimate/work plan. The AI doesn’t replace the PM; it produces faster, clearer decisions with confidence explanations.

3) Market + customers

Primary customer (ICP): US-based small property management firms with 50–500 doors that need operational efficiency and better owner/tenant experience, but don’t have enterprise budgets or internal tooling teams.

Buyer personas:

  • Owner/Principal: cares about profitability, owner retention, and reducing chaos.
  • Operations/Property Manager: cares about task flow, communication, and "nothing falls through the cracks".
  • Maintenance coordinator: cares about scope clarity, vendor scheduling, and costs.

4) Competitive landscape (high level)

  • Incumbents: AppFolio / Buildium / Propertyware / Rent Manager / DoorLoop / TenantCloud (varies by segment).
  • Differentiation strategy: (1) inspection-centered workflow, (2) superior UX and speed, (3) AI that explains itself and reduces uncertainty, (4) pragmatic integrations (Stripe + QuickBooks) to fit existing back offices.

6) AI strategy (pragmatic startup)

Phase 1: AI-assisted estimate/work plan (inspection-driven)

  • Input: inspection checklist items with structured fields + notes + photos (optional).
  • Output: line items (repair/replace/investigate), labor/material ranges, assumptions, and questions to reduce uncertainty.
  • UX principle: explainability first (confidence + rationale visible).

Phase 2: predictive risk scoring

  • Use historical inspection + maintenance + asset ages to predict likely failures within horizon (e.g., 60 days).
  • Deliverable: ranked “watch list” by property/unit/asset with recommended preventative actions.

7) Monetization + pricing (placeholders)

Needs your guidance. Below is a workable starting structure for small PM firms.

  • Core platform: $X / door / month with a minimum monthly fee (e.g., $199–$499).
  • Add-ons: SMS bundles, e-sign envelopes, advanced AI estimate runs, premium support.

8) Distribution + sales motion

  • Initial channels: local network + cold outreach to PM principals/ops managers.
  • Motion: hybrid (demo-led for first customers; self-serve later).
  • Proof: show “inspection → estimate” workflow live; quantify time saved and reduced variance.

9) Operations plan (solo founder reality)

  • Prioritize reliability and tight workflows over feature breadth.
  • Use integrations for payments/accounting rather than rebuilding.
  • Support: start high-touch (concierge) to learn; document repeatable onboarding.

10) Milestones to $10k MRR (example)

  1. Demo-ready MVP: inspection detail + action fields + estimate generation + history.
  2. Beta (2–5 firms): weekly feedback loops; track time-to-complete inspection and time-to-scope maintenance.
  3. v1 Launch: billing + QBO sync stable; leasing/docs usable; automations “minimum viable”.
  4. Scale to $10k MRR: 10–20 firms onboarded (depends on doors/pricing); introduce onboarding package + add-ons.

11) Risks + mitigations

  • Scope creep: lock v1 workflows; delay nice-to-haves.
  • Trust in AI outputs: always show assumptions, ranges, and questions; allow edits and approvals.
  • Integration complexity: start with Stripe + QuickBooks; add more only after stable.
  • Solo bandwidth: strict weekly plan; ship in small increments; avoid rewrites.
