# Black Atlas Productions — Business Plan v1

## 1) Executive Summary
Black Atlas Productions is a Wichita-based independent production company focused on live events, stage, and film. The business model starts with high-impact, brand-cohesive live programming (Black Atlas Live), then expands into stage and film projects using the same artist network, production systems, and audience base.

## 2) Problem & Opportunity
### Problem
- Local artists often lack paid, high-quality production opportunities.
- Cultural programming is fragmented and inconsistent.
- Independent teams lack repeatable systems and documentation.

### Opportunity
- Build a premium-feeling local production brand with disciplined execution.
- Capture underserved demand for original, high-craft events.
- Convert event audiences into recurring community and future buyers.

## 3) Vision-Aligned Business Model
## Primary Revenue Streams (Year 1)
1. Ticket sales from Black Atlas Live events
2. Sponsorship packages (Title/Gold/Silver/Friends/Custom)
3. Grants (arts, cultural development, workforce/creative economy)
4. Service revenue (select production services/creative direction)

## Secondary Revenue Streams (Year 2+)
1. Co-productions and presenter partnerships
2. Educational intensives/workshops
3. Branded merchandise
4. Licensing/distribution revenue from film projects

## 4) Target Market
### Primary
- 18+ culturally engaged audiences in Wichita seeking premium, original experiences
- Local brands/businesses seeking authentic sponsorship alignment
- Funders prioritizing arts access, workforce development, and local impact

### Secondary
- Regional audiences and collaborators
- Venue and institutional partners
- Artists seeking a high-standard, repeatable production ecosystem

## 5) Offer Portfolio
1. **Black Atlas Live** (flagship): 3-event seasonal arc
2. **Black Atlas Stage**: workshop-to-performance development pathway
3. **Black Atlas Film**: short/microbudget pipeline seeded by live/stage IP
4. **Brand/Production Services** (select): concept, show flow, creative production support

## 6) Go-to-Market
- Brand-led campaigns with strong visual identity
- Artist-feature storytelling content (build affinity + trust)
- Partner amplification through venues/sponsors/community organizations
- Post-event recap content to drive social proof and conversion

## 7) Operations Plan
### Core Functions
- Producing & programming
- Creative direction
- Production management
- Marketing/content
- Partnerships/sponsorships
- Finance/admin/reporting

### Operating Rhythm
- Weekly production meeting
- Weekly pipeline + sales review
- Monthly financial review
- Post-event 72-hour debrief + systems update

## 8) Financial Model (Year 1 Starter)
### Target Envelope
- Season 1 operating budget: ~$18,000 (3 events)

### Revenue Mix Target
- Grants: 50–60%
- Sponsorships/donations: 20–30%
- Ticket sales: 15–25%
- In-kind support: supplemental

### Margin Strategy
- Protect quality while reducing avoidable costs through templates, partner deals, and reusable production assets.

## 9) Key Risks & Mitigations
1. **Cash flow timing risk** -> staged payment schedules + contingency reserve
2. **Attendance risk** -> stronger presale strategy + partner distribution
3. **Execution overload** -> strict scope control + repeatable runbooks
4. **Sponsor conversion risk** -> tiered offers + measurable reporting proof

## 10) KPIs (What We Track)
### Business Health
- Monthly cash in/out
- Revenue by stream
- Sponsorship pipeline value and conversion rate

### Program Performance
- Attendance and sell-through
- Paid artist/crew roles and payout totals
- Audience impact score (inspired/changed/moved)

### Brand Growth
- Email list growth
- Social engagement and reach
- Partner retention/renewal

## 11) 12-Month Milestones
- Q1: lock strategy, sponsorship assets, first grants submitted
- Q2: production prep + sales launch + partner activations
- Q3: execute Event 1 and Event 2
- Q4: execute Event 3 + full reporting + Season 2 plan

## 12) Decision Rules (to stay aligned)
- Does it strengthen the Black Atlas brand thesis?
- Does it create paid pathways and measurable impact?
- Does it improve long-term infrastructure, not just short-term activity?
- If no to 2+ questions: decline, defer, or redesign.
