## 1) How to Use This Book
- **If you have 20–30 min:** run a short warmup, one core drill, one song section, and log one metric.
- **If you have 45–75 min:** follow the full 7-day framework and end with one recorded output artifact.
- **Always leave with evidence:** a take, a checklist, or a scored rubric.
- **Focus rotation rule:** Foundation → Stamina → Expression → Performance.

## 2) Daily Execution Flow (10/20/30 Minute Warmups)
### 10-Minute Reset
1. Breath reset (1 min)
2. Gentle SOVT/lip trill (2 min)
3. Mix bridge prep on "mum" or "ng→ah" (3 min)
4. Song phrase primer (2 min)
5. One target line at tempo (2 min)

### 20-Minute Builder
1. 10-minute reset
2. Add articulation timing drill (4 min)
3. Add resonance placement pass by section (4 min)
4. One mini performance run (2 min)

### 30-Minute Performance Prep
1. 20-minute builder
2. Chorus endurance/intensity ladder (5 min)
3. One full section simulation + notes (5 min)

## 3) Weekly Progression System
Use this default cycle each week:
- **Day 1:** Foundation reset (breath, onset, posture)
- **Day 2:** Belt stamina / support management
- **Day 3:** Tone + emotional contrast
- **Day 4:** Integration session (transitions, sequencing)
- **Day 5:** Resonance consistency
- **Day 6:** Performance simulation (single-pass)
- **Day 7:** Review + next-week programming

Milestones to unlock harder repertoire:
- 2 clean takes on hard moments
- Stable breath plan over full run
- Consistent vowel strategy on sustained notes
- One recorded performance-quality take

# Song Workup Protocol

Purpose: Standard process for turning a new song choice into a performance-ready repertoire item.

## Trigger phrase
`new song: <title> by <artist>`

## Workflow
1. Create a new folder (or note set) for the song.
2. Duplicate `song-breakdown-template.md` and fill it fully.
3. Produce three outputs:
   - Song brief
   - Drill sheet
   - 7-day micro plan
4. Add task entries to Notion:
   - Analyze song
   - Warm-up/drill sessions
   - Performance simulation
   - Review and lock version
5. Schedule practice blocks on calendar (if requested).

## Required sections to complete every time
- Musical breakdown
- Lyric/interpretation map
- Vowel strategy
- Resonance analysis (required)
- Technical risk map
- Warm-up sequence
- Tough-area exercises
- Rehearsal ladder

## Exercise design rules
- Tie each exercise to a specific line/moment.
- Start at easier coordination (SOVT, hum, lip trill, NG) before full lyric.
- Use tempo progression: 60% → 75% → 90% → 100%.
- Keep drills short, specific, repeatable.
- Include at least one resonance-placement drill for each major hard section.

## Quality gate (definition of done)
A song is “rep-ready” when:
1. Hard moments have at least 2 successful clean takes.
2. Breath plan is stable across full run.
3. Vowel strategy is consistent on sustained/high notes.
4. Interpretation choices are intentional and repeatable.
5. One full performance take is recorded and reviewed.

## Output naming convention
- `song-brief-<slug>.md`
- `drill-sheet-<slug>.md`
- `7-day-plan-<slug>.md`

Example slug: `the-show-must-go-on`

## Optional scoring rubric (1–5)
- Pitch stability
- Breath management
- Diction clarity
- Emotional communication
- Stamina/endurance

Log scores each week to track growth.

# Song Brief — Survivor (Destiny’s Child)

## 0) Song Intake
- **Song Title:** Survivor
- **Artist / Version:** Destiny’s Child (original studio version)
- **Performance Goal:** Repertoire + performance confidence anthem
- **Target Date:** 7-day workup then stage-ready polish

---

## 1) Musical Breakdown
- **Tonal center / key:** Commonly performed around E minor (varies by arrangement/transposition)
- **Feel:** Mid-tempo, assertive pop/R&B groove
- **Form map:** Intro → Verse 1 → Pre/transition lift → Chorus → Verse 2 → Chorus → Bridge/statement section → Final chorus/outro
- **Range (practical):** Moderate-high female pop belt territory; key challenge is sustained power + speech-like clarity, not extreme top notes
- **Tessitura:** Sits in a strong chest/mix zone for long stretches
- **Rhythmic challenges:** Tight consonant timing on punchy lyric lines; staying locked to beat while keeping attitude
- **Phrasing challenges:** Sustained intensity without over-pushing early

---

## 2) Lyrical + Story Analysis
- **Core theme:** Resilience, self-definition, post-breakup empowerment
- **Speaker POV:** Direct, grounded, unbothered, victorious
- **Emotional arc:**
  - **Verse:** factual, no-nonsense recounting
  - **Chorus:** declaration/identity statement
  - **Bridge/final section:** expanded confidence + proof of growth
- **Interpretation note:** Deliver as confident truth, not angry shouting. The power is control.

---

## 3) Vowel / Diction Strategy
- **Key sustained words:** “survivor,” “stronger,” “harder,” “wiser,” “fighter”
- **Vowel strategy:**
  - Keep bright-forward vowels but avoid spread (especially on higher sustained notes)
  - Slightly round “ah/uh” shapes when intensity rises
- **Consonants:** Crisp on downbeats; don’t over-hit final consonants and choke airflow
- **Rule:** Text clarity + groove first, then belt weight

---

## 4) Resonance Analysis
- **Resonance map by section:**
  - **Verse:** speech-forward chest/mix resonance, clear text-first tone
  - **Pre/transition:** slightly lifted mask resonance to prep chorus intensity
  - **Chorus:** firm chest-dominant mix with forward ring (avoid throat push)
  - **Final section/outro:** maintain ring while slightly widening emotional color, not volume-only escalation
- **Placement targets:**
  - Main target = forward oral resonance with stable laryngeal freedom
  - Keep sternum energy without pressing the root of tongue
  - On repeated hooks, think “spin forward” instead of “push harder”
- **Vowel-resonance pairing:**
  - “survivor” / “fighter” — keep bright core, slightly round if pitch rises
  - “stronger” / “harder” — avoid over-wide spread; maintain vertical space
  - Long declarations — anchor breath + release jaw to keep resonance stable
- **Trouble-note resonance fixes:**
  - If chorus tightens: run line on "ng-ah" then restore lyric
  - If final section gets shouty: switch to "mum" pass at 70% then rebuild
  - If diction kills tone: speak rhythm with buoyant airflow, then re-sing
- **Tone goal references:**
  - Verses: confident, conversational steel
  - Choruses: bright resilient ring
  - Final pass: triumphant without strain (controlled intensity)

## 5) Technical Risk Map
- **Risk 1:** Over-belting early choruses → fatigue by final section
- **Risk 2:** Jaw/tongue tension from aggressive diction
- **Risk 3:** Breath compression during rapid lyric lines
- **Breath landmarks:** Plan quick replenishment before each chorus entry and before final repeated declarations

**Top 3 hard moments**
1. Repeated chorus declarations at consistent energy
2. Fast lyric passages requiring articulation + groove
3. Final section stamina while preserving pitch center

---

## 5) Song-Specific Warm-Up (8–12 min)
1. **Breath reset (90 sec):** 4-in / 6-out, relaxed ribs
2. **SOVT (2 min):** lip trill slides low-mid-high-mid
3. **Mix bridge (2–3 min):** “mum-mum-mum” on 5-tone, medium volume
4. **Articulation prep (2 min):** “d-g-b” rhythmic patterns at song tempo feel
5. **Phrase primer (2 min):** speak-then-sing chorus at 70% intensity

---

## 6) Tough-Area Exercise Pairing
### A) Chorus stamina
- **Exercise 1:** Octave “yeah/hey” pulses at medium volume (8 reps)
- **Exercise 2:** Chorus melody on “ng→ah” to release jaw pressure

### B) Fast lyric clarity
- **Exercise 1:** Rhythm-only spoken text with metronome
- **Exercise 2:** Staccato sing-through on “da” then restore lyrics

### C) Final section endurance
- **Exercise 1:** 3-pass ladder (60% / 80% / 100% intensity)
- **Exercise 2:** Interval breath training between repeated hooks

---

## 7) Performance Direction
- **Character:** grounded winner, not frantic
- **Dynamics:** build confidence curve across song; save max edge for final chorus
- **Body language cue:** stable stance + intentional forward focus on declarations

---

## 8) Rehearsal Ladder
1. Text + groove only
2. Melody clean with easy vowels
3. Full lyric with controlled intensity
4. Performance pass with emotional commitment
5. One-take simulation + review

# Drill Sheet — Survivor (Destiny’s Child)

## Hard Moment 1: Chorus repeats lose quality by final pass
- **Likely cause:** too much push on first two choruses
- **Drill A (slow):** chorus on "mum" at 65% intensity, focus on consistent breath flow
- **Drill B (tempo):** full chorus x3 with intensity ladder (70/85/100)
- **Re-test line:** first and last chorus declarations back-to-back

## Hard Moment 2: Fast lyric lines get muddy
- **Likely cause:** tongue/jaw tension + consonants landing late
- **Drill A (slow):** speak text in rhythm with metronome (no pitch)
- **Drill B (tempo):** sing line staccato on "da-da-da," then restore lyrics
- **Re-test line:** one full verse with crisp consonant starts

## Hard Moment 3: Final section feels breath-starved
- **Likely cause:** shallow replenishment between phrases
- **Drill A (slow):** breath checkpoint practice (mark all quick inhale spots)
- **Drill B (tempo):** final section with planned silent quick-breaths
- **Re-test line:** final repeated declaration sequence clean + steady

1. Lip trill siren x3
2. "mum" 5-tone x3

## 7) Performance Readiness Checklist
### 24 Hours Before
- Hydration + sleep protected
- No heavy vocal loading late in day
- Confirm key, arrangement, cue points
### Day Of
- Run 10–20 minute warmup only (don’t oversing)
- Test hardest line once, then stop drilling
- Review lyric intention map (not just notes)
### 10 Minutes Pre-Stage
- 2 breath resets + 2 resonance glides + 1 confidence phrase
- Lock first line tone and tempo internally
- Commit to storytelling over perfection

## 8) Troubleshooting by Symptom
- **Tight throat on chorus:** switch to `ng→ah` pass, reduce intensity to 70%, reintroduce lyric gradually.
- **Mudgy fast lyrics:** rhythm-speak with metronome, staccato on `da`, then restore words.
- **Breath collapse by final section:** mark inhale checkpoints and train silent quick replenishments.
- **Pitch drift on sustained notes:** narrow vowel spread, add vertical space, reduce jaw pressure.
- **Fatigue early in run:** use intensity ladder (60→75→90→100) instead of full-force first pass.
